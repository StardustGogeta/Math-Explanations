Derivative of Cosine
As per the definition of a derivative,
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\cos(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{\cos(x+h)-\cos(x)}{h}`
Simplify with the cosine angle addition formula:
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\cos(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{\cos(x)\cos(h)-\sin(h)\sin(x)-\cos(x)}{h}`
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\cos(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{\cos(x)(\cos(h)-1)-\sin(h)\sin(x)}{h}`
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\cos(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{\cos(h)-1}{h}\cos(x)-\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\sin(x)`
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\cos(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{\cos(h)-1}{h}\frac{\cos(h)+1}{\cos(h)+1}\cos(x)-\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\sin(x)`
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\cos(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{\cos^2(h)-1}{h(\cos(h)+1)}\cos(x)-\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\sin(x)`
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\cos(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{-\sin^2(h)}{h(\cos(h)+1)}\cos(x)-\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\sin(x)`
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\cos(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\frac{-\sin(h)}{\cos(h)+1}\cos(x)-\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\sin(x)`
Using `\lim_{x\to0}\frac{\sin(x)}{x}=1`:
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\cos(x)=1\cdot\frac02\cdot\cos(x)-1\cdot\sin(x)`
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\cos(x)=-\sin(x)`
