Quotient Rule
There are two methods: use the product rule only or add the chain rule.
Product Rule
Start with the product rule equation, where `u` and `v` are differentiable functions of `x`:
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}(uv) = \frac{\mathrm{d}u}{\mathrm{d}x} \cdot v + u \cdot \frac{\mathrm{d}v}{\mathrm{d}x}`
Now, given that `f=\frac{g}{h}`, `g=f\cdot h`,
`g' = f'h + fh'`
`f'h = g' - fh'`
`f' = \frac{g'-fh'}{h}`
`f' = \frac{g'-\frac{g}{h}h'}{h}`
`f' = \frac{g'h-gh'}{h^2}`
Product Rule and Chain Rule
Alternatively,
`\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}(uv) = \frac{\mathrm{d}u}{\mathrm{d}x} \cdot v + u \cdot \frac{\mathrm{d}v}{\mathrm{d}x}`
Given that `f=g\cdot \frac{1}{h}`,
`f' = g'\cdot \frac{1}{h} + g\cdot(-\frac{1}{h^2}\cdot h')`,
`f' = \frac{g'}{h}-\frac{gh'}{h^2}`,
Finally,
`f' = \frac{g'h-gh'}{h^2}`,
