Derivative of Sine
As per the definition of a derivative,
`\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x)=\lim_{h\rightarrow0}\frac{\sin(x+h)-\sin(x)}{h}`
Simplify with the sine angle addition formula:
`\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x)=\lim_{h\rightarrow0}\frac{\sin(x)\cos(h)+\sin(h)\cos(x)-\sin(x)}{h}`
`\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x)=\lim_{h\rightarrow0}\frac{\sin(x)(\cos(h)-1)+\sin(h)\cos(x)}{h}`
`\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x)=\lim_{h\rightarrow0}\frac{\cos(h)-1}{h}\sin(x)+\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\cos(x)`
`\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x)=\lim_{h\rightarrow0}\frac{\cos(h)-1}{h}\frac{\cos(h)+1}{\cos(h)+1}\sin(x)+\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\cos(x)`
`\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x)=\lim_{h\rightarrow0}\frac{\cos^2(h)-1}{h(\cos(h)+1)}\sin(x)+\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\cos(x)`
`\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x)=\lim_{h\rightarrow0}\frac{-\sin^2(h)}{h(\cos(h)+1)}\sin(x)+\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\cos(x)`
`\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x)=\lim_{h\rightarrow0}\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\frac{-\sin(h)}{\cos(h)+1}\sin(x)+\frac{\sin(h)}{h}\cos(x)`
Using `\lim_{x\rightarrow0}\frac{\sin(x)}{x}=1`:
`\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x)=1\cdot\frac02\cdot\sin(x)+1\cdot\cos(x)`
`\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x)=\cos(x)`
