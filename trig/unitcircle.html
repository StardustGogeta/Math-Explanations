Unit Circle Trigonometric Ratio Definitions

Given any angle `\theta`, the basic trigonometric ratios can all be defined geometrically on the circle of radius `1`.
`sin(x)` is the side of the small triangle opposite the angle.
`cos(x)` is the side of the small triangle adjacent to the angle.
`tan(x)` is the side of the large triangle opposite the angle.
`csc(x)` is the hypotenuse of the large, bottom triangle.
`sec(x)` is the hypotenuse of the large, top triangle.
`cot(x)` is the side of the large, bottom triangle opposite the angle..
