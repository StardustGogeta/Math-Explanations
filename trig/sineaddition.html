Sine Angle Addition
NOTE: Proof taken from Khan Academy.
Observe the red angle, `\angle x`, and the blue angle, `\angle y`.
The hypotenuse of `\angle (x+y)` is given as `1`.
Hence, the length of the green line represents `sin(x+y)`.
The bottom part of the line, beneath the horizontal, is given by
`cos(x)sin(y)`, as seen on the opposite edge of the rectangle.
The upper portion is equal to the value of `sin(x)cos(y)`,
as given by the triangle formed in the upper-right.
Altogether, `sin(x+y)=sin(x)cos(y)+cos(x)sin(y)`.
