Cosine Angle Addition
NOTE: Proof taken from Khan Academy.
Observe the red angle, `\angle x`, and the blue angle, `\angle y`.
The hypotenuse of `\angle (x+y)` is given as `1`.
Hence, the length of the horizontal blue line that is
left of the green vertical represents `\cos(x+y)`.
The complete length of the line is given by `\cos(x)\cos(y)`.
The part on the right is `\sin(x)\sin(y)`, as given by
the triangle in the upper-right corner.
Therefore, the part of the blue line left of the green is equal to
`\cos(x+y)=\cos(x)\cos(y)-\sin(x)\sin(y)`
