Tangent Angle Addition
Recall the sine addition and cosine addition formulas:
`\sin(x+y)=\sin(x)\cos(y)+\cos(x)\sin(y)`
`\cos(x+y)=\cos(x)\cos(y)-\sin(x)\sin(y)`
Also note the quotient identity: `\tan(x)=\frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x)}`.
Using these three equations, one can determine that
`\tan(x+y)=\frac{\sin(x+y)}{\cos(x+y)}`
`\tan(x+y)=\frac{\sin(x)\cos(y)+\cos(x)\sin(y)}{\cos(x)\cos(y)-\sin(x)\sin(y)}`
`\tan(x+y)=\frac{\frac{\sin(x)\cos(y)}{\cos(x)\cos(y)}+\frac{\cos(x)\sin(y)}{\cos(x)\cos(y)}}{\frac{\cos(x)\cos(y)}{\cos(x)\cos(y)}-\frac{\sin(x)\sin(y)}{\cos(x)\cos(y)}}`
`\tan(x+y)=\frac{\frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x)}+\frac{\sin(y)}{\cos(y)}}{1-\frac{\sin(x)\sin(y)}{\cos(x)\cos(y)}}`
`\tan(x+y)=\frac{\tan(x)+\tan(y)}{1-\tan(x)\tan(y)}`
