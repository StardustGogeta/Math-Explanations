Power-Reducing Identities
Sine Power-Reduction
Recall the pythagorean identity:
`\sin^2(x)+\cos^2(x)=1`
`\sin^2(x)=1-\cos^2(x)`
`2\sin^2(x)=1-(\cos^2(x)-\sin^2(x))`
Recall the double angle formula:
`2\sin^2(x)=1-\cos(2x)`
`\sin^2(x)=\frac{1-\cos(2x)}{2}`
Cosine Power-Reduction
Recall the pythagorean identity:
`\sin^2(x)+\cos^2(x)=1`
`\cos^2(x)=1-\sin^2(x)`
`2\cos^2(x)=1+(\cos^2(x)-\sin^2(x))`
Recall the double angle formula:
`2\cos^2(x)=1+\cos(2x)`
`\cos^2(x)=\frac{1+\cos(2x)}{2}`
Tangent Power-Reduction
Recall the power-reduction formulas:
`\sin^2(x)=\frac{1-\cos(2x)}{2}`
`\cos^2(x)=\frac{1+\cos(2x)}{2}`
Also note the quotient identity:
`\tan(x)=\frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x)} \to \tan^2(x)=\frac{\sin^2(x)}{\cos^2(x)}`.
`\tan^2(x)=\frac{1-\cos(2x)}{1+\cos(2x)}`
