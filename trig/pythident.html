Pythagorean Identities
Observe the unit circle below, where the radius is `1`.

The vertical line is of length `\sin(x)`, and the horizontal is of length `\cos(x)`.
These form a right triangle with hypotenuse `1`, revealing the first Pythagorean identity:
`\sin^2(x)+\cos^2(x)=1`
Divide by `\sin^2(x)` to obtain another:
`1+\cot^2(x)=\csc^2(x)`
Finally, divide the original by `\cos^2(x)`:
`\tan^2(x)+1=\sec^2(x)`
