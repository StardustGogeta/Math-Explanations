Quadratic Formula
Start with the equation for a quadratic function:
`ax^2+bx+c=0`
There are now two options: derive, or solve algebraically.
Derivative
`\frac{d}{dx}(ax^2+bx+c)=0`
`2ax_{vertex}+b=0`
`2ax_{vertex} = -b`
`x_{vertex} = -\frac{b}{2a}`
`ax^2+bx+c=0`, and from the vertex, `y_{vertex}+au^2=0`
where `u` is the horizontal distance to each root.
`\frac{b^2}{4a}-\frac{b^2}{2a}+c=y_{vertex}`
`-\frac{b^2}{4a}+\frac{4ac}{4a}=-au^2`
`\frac{b^2-4ac}{4a^2}=u^2`
`\frac{\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}=u`
Hence, `x=\frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}`
Algebra
`ax^2+bx=-c`
`x^2+\frac{b}{a}x=-\frac{c}{a}`
`x^2+\frac{b}{a}x+\frac{b^2}{4a^2}=\frac{b^2}{4a^2}-\frac{c}{a}`
`(x+\frac{b}{2a})^2=\frac{b^2-4ac}{4a^2}`
`x+\frac{b}{2a}=\frac{\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}`
`x=\frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}`
